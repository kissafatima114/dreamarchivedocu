Concept

Memory as an Unstable Space

Dream Archive explores how memories behave less like fixed records and more like shifting environments. The project focuses on the experience of remembering rather than the accuracy of what is remembered. Familiar landscape spaces such as skies, bridges, and open fields were chosen because they feel recognizable but difficult to locate precisely, allowing them to function as emotional memory environments rather than specific places.

The structure of the archive suggests preservation and organization, but the instability of the images challenges this expectation. Instead of presenting memories clearly, the interface allows them to blur, distort, and overlap as the viewer moves through the site. Navigation is intentionally minimal so that visitors explore the archive intuitively rather than following a fixed sequence.

By treating the website itself as a memory space, Dream Archive invites viewers to experience memory as something that shifts over time rather than something that can be stored permanently.
